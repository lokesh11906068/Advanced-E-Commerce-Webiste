AGRICULTURE PRACTICES

  • Agriculture is the main occupation. It provides employment to nearly 61% persons of total population.
  • Agriculture in India mainly depends on monsoon. If monsoon is good, the production will be more and if monsoon is less than average then the crops fail.
  • Due is increase in population the pressure on land holding increased. Land holdings get fragmentated and subdivided and become uneconomical.
  • Due to inadequate irrigation facilities and uncertain rainfall, the production of agriculture is less, farmers find work a few months in the year.
  • Due to large scale sub-division and fragmentation of holdings, land holding size is quite small. Average size of land holding was 2.3 hectares in India while in Australia it was 1993 hectares and in USA it was 158 hectares.

    SUBSISTANCE FARMING

    Subsistence farming, form of farming in which nearly all of the crops or livestock raised are used to maintain the farmer and the farmer's family, leaving little, if any, surplus for sale or trade. Preindustrial agricultural peoples throughout the world have traditionally practiced subsistence farming

    COMMERCIAL GRAIN FARMING

    Commercial grain farming has successfully developed in economically developed tem­perate region where population density is low, availability of land is high and only a small fraction of the population is dependent on agricultural activities.

    ARABLE FARMING

    Arable farming includes growing crops like wheat and barley instead of growing vegetables and fruits or keeping animals. It is used to accomplish the rising demand of food. It can be performed mainly in small scale, commercial or on large agricultural farms.

    NOMADIC HERDING

    Nomadic Herding – the wandering, but controlled movement of livestock, solely dependent on natural forage – is the most extensive type of land use system. Sheep and goats are the most common with cattle, horses and yaks locally important

    AGRI-EQUIPMENTS

    • An organic farm requires less capital investment than a conventional farm, since it does not require the purchase of chemical fertilizers.

    • Depending on the conditions, organic farming methods may yield less than conventional methods.

    • The environment benefits from organic farming in many ways. Avoiding harsh chemicals allows for better biodiversity

    • Organic farming thrives on diversity. This keeps the soil rich, which means good crops will continue to grow year after year.
